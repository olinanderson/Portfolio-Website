Van Conversion Before

  • Olin Anderson
  • Design and Building

The first step in building this van conversion was to source a van. When researching the endless brands and configurations of cargo vans, I settled on the Ford Transit high roof extended length (engine didn't matter to me at this point). This is because I am 6'4" tall and needed the height if I wanted to stand up inside. There was a local contractor that lives in my neighborhood which had the exact van I was looking for. I found his company email, emailed him, and eventually went through with the purchase.

2016 Ford Transit 3.5L Ecoboost, High Roof, Extended Length

The van had an aluminum partition with a bench seat in the cab. The van is has a 3.5L EcoBoost® V6. I'm glad that it ended up being an EcoBoost® because of the power. This is what I started the build from:

I started by taking the metal partition and bench out, stripping all side walls and roof, and ripping up the carpet where the bench was. this showed the old plywood covering on the floor, which I also had to rip up. Once this was done, the empty cargo shell was left to start the actual build process on.

Build Process

  • Olin Anderson
  • Construction

The van came with spray foam insulation, which I ended up really liking, as it's great for sound and temperature. My first plan of action was to install the roof fan, gasoline heater, and solar panels as winter was approaching and this would allow me to work on the interior in the winter. I carefully cut a square hole in the roof and made sure the fan mounted and fit properly before marking and drilling holes.

The floor was slightly rusty as it had been sitting underneath plywood for a 4 years. The next step was to vacuum the floor, wash it with a rust remover solution, plug any screw holes, then paint over with white paint. After the floor was prepped, it was time to spread plywood between the ribs on the floor, to ensure that we're keeping as much height of the van as possible.

After the plywood had been glued between the ribs, the next step was to apply rigid foam insulation. Since there were contours around the wheel wells I needed to cut out some cardboard templates, then I was able to cut out the foam insulation to fit perfectly.

Once the rigid foam insulation was glued down, there was one layer of plywood left to do. Once the last layer of plywood is laid down, it's time to start placing the vinyl flooring.

Now that the floor is installed and finished, the next step was to build a partition from the cab to the living space, start framing out the wall, and install the electrical compartment. I wanted to build a partition for thermal and privacy reasons as well.

The shower was framed out with a small overhang over the door step to provide clearance for the drain so that the recirculating shower is all on the interior, which means that the van is 4 seasons capable, as none of the water lines will freeze in the winter as long as the heater is running in the van.

The shower was tiled with vinyl plank tiles that are tongue and groove and fully water proof. Before the tiles were glued on the walls, I installed a water membrane called schluter-kerdi. This is to ensure that any water that gets behind the tiles isn't going to rot the framing of the shower.

After/Current State of Van Build

The state of the van build is listed below:

The following photos show the current state (as of Oct 20th, 2021) of the van conversion.

The only propane appliances in the van are the stove top and the on-demand water heater. All the propane lines were done with flexible copper pipe and flare fittings. The water heater is placed above the kitchen unit and is fed off of the propane line as well.

For the fresh water system, I chose to have one water heater and have it switch ball valves around so I can flush the shower, and change the heater from the sink to the closed-loop shower. Overall turns both water pumps off and all LEDs off, recirc switches the hot water to the shower, else switches the hot water to the sink and outdoor spigot, and the flush button switches a few ball valves around so that the recirc-pump pushes water into the grey water tank, and the else-pump pushes water from the fresh tank into the shower (i.e. flushes the dirty water out of the recirculating shower).
